# CONTENT PROGRESSION PLAN

**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Horizon:** Beta and beyond

---

## Design Principle

No more percentage bonuses. No more stat upgrades.

Every level unlock must be a new situation: a new customer type, a new event, a new thing that can go wrong or right. The player's skill grows because the world gets richer, not because their numbers get bigger.

## Beta Content Plan (v2.1–v2.4)

### Level 9 Content Unlock: "REGULAR CUSTOMERS"
A handful of customers appear more than once across sessions. When a regular arrives:
- Name banner reads their name (e.g., "James — Regular")
- They have a remembered preference (always orders the same item)
- Serve them their preference without taking an order → bonus points
- If you get it wrong → they're disappointed (small penalty)
- Over time, the player "knows" 3-5 regulars

This creates attachment. "James is here — he always orders the burger."

### Level 10 Content Unlock: "THE HEALTH INSPECTOR"
Not a food critic. A health inspector.
- Arrives unannounced, 1 per 3 sessions at Level 10
- They watch a specific mechanic (dirty tables, speed, combo)
- If the restaurant is in good shape when they leave: "PASSED INSPECTION" — session-wide +20% score
- If a dirty table is visible when they observe: "FAILED INSPECTION" — session ends 30s early
- Creates paranoia that makes every dirty table feel urgent

## Content After Beta

### Second Restaurant: "THE ROOFTOP BAR"
- New aesthetic: open air, sunset sky, city backdrop
- New mechanics: drink orders (no kitchen — player mixes at bar counter), weather events
- 5 new food items: cocktails, tapas, cheese board, bruschetta, mini desserts
- New customer type: "Sunset Photographer" — wants a specific table (window)
- Connects to Level 5 unlock (player has proven they can handle the critic)

### Third Restaurant: "THE FAST FOOD COUNTER"
- Speed over quality: orders go out fast but patience is brutally short
- New mechanic: assembly line (player picks components from stations)
- New customer type: "Drive-through voice" who never appears in person
- High volume, low drama — a different feel entirely

### Fourth Restaurant: "THE PRIVATE DINING ROOM"
- Only 2 tables, but VIP customers only
- Every customer is a food critic-level challenge
- New mechanic: "Course Pairing" — wrong food with wrong drink = penalty
- Unlocks at Level 7 (player has VIP experience)

## Permanent Special Events (Post-Beta)

These occur randomly across all restaurants, independent of session type:

| Event | Trigger | Mechanic |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Power Outage | Random, 1/20 sessions | Kitchen offline for 60s — only cold dishes available |
| Special Occasion | Two customers at different tables | Both order same last dish — one gets it, one is disappointed |
| The Big Group | 4 customers arrive together | All want the same table (impossible) — player must split them |
| Wrong Order Kitchen | Random, 1/15 sessions | One kitchen order comes out for the wrong table |
| Fire Alarm Drill | Random, 1/25 sessions | Venue clears for 20s — score continues accumulating based on service quality before |

---

## Seasonal Content (Live Operations)

After commercial launch, monthly events keep players returning:

- **Valentine's Day**: all tables decorated, heart-shaped food, every customer arrives as a couple
- **Christmas Rush**: tables decorated, customers in holiday mood (more patient), but 2x customers arrive
- **Restaurant Week**: week-long event where serving 100 customers unlocks a permanent restaurant upgrade
- **Chef Challenge**: weekly challenge with specific constraints ("serve 5 VIPs in one session")

---

## What We Will NOT Build

- More percentage bonuses on existing mechanics
- Stat trees or talent trees
- Pay-to-win time skips
- Gacha mechanics on content unlocks
- Story content that requires reading more than 2 sentences

The game is a reflex game with narrative decoration. The narrative serves the reflex, not the other way around.
